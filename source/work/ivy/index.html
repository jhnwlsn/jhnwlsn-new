Background

Ivy is an online publishing and interactive training platform. Everyone from startups to large enterprises can use Ivy to create their own technical courses. Throughout 2015 and 2016, I designed and developed the marketing site and was a designer for the "player" layer of the platform.

The concept for Ivy was born out of Envy's successes with Code School. Online learning was becoming an important part of the tech industry, and Envy found itself building a similar concept over-and-over again based off of Code School. Thus, creating a reusable platform would decrease client's costs while increase Envy's productivity.

Longform Content

Taking cues from both Code School and several other previous clients, we decided to build the structure for a single course around longform formatted content.

By formatting the content into this structure, it limits unneccessary interuptions and helps the student stay focused and truly learn and retain from what is being communicated. Upon entering the course through the table of contents, the student is presented with an interface that is both clean and non-distracting, which allows them to focus on the course.

Interactive Console

Where the real value of Ivy exists is in the interactive coding console. It allows students to create and test code all while remaining in their browser.

While the student is reading the longform content, eventually they will come to a new section of content that signifies an interacitve console challenge made up of tasks. Once the user intializes the challenge, the console simply slides in and is ready to use, keeping the interface simple and easy to understand.

Final Encouragement

Once a student has completed any task or challenge, we needed to tie it all together and keep the user engaged until the very end of the course. We did this in two ways: gifting points and positive messaging for successfully completed tasks and challenges. While the points within Ivy don't mean much outside of an integer, they can easily be tied into any external pipeline just as the rest of Ivy's features.
